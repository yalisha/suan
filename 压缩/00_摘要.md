# 摘要

## 中文摘要

算法推荐系统在优化用户体验的同时也引发了信息茧房、注意力垄断等问题，如何有效治理算法成为全球数字治理的核心议题。本文利用2024年11月12日中国清朗行动算法治理政策这一准自然实验，采用断点回归时间序列（ITS）与双重差分（DID）相结合的方法，基于微博热搜榜2024年2月至2025年10月超过253,227条观测值的高频数据，系统评估了算法治理对平台生态系统的重构效应。研究发现：（1）算法治理显著促进注意力分布去中心化，Shannon熵上升18.7%，HHI下降11.8%；（2）分级分类治理导致内容质量分化，社会类话题信息差下降23.1%，娱乐类保持稳定；（3）在注意力零和约束下，监管引发社会类向娱乐类的系统性注意力再分配，社会类份额下降3.16个百分点，娱乐类上升3.84个百分点，呈现明显的"水床效应"；（4）注意力密度在类别间显著分化，社会类呈"少而精"，娱乐类呈"多且长"。本研究提出"元组织的元组织"和"水床效应"理论机制，拓展了平台治理理论边界，为理解算法治理的系统性后果和非预期效应提供了新证据，对完善分级分类治理体系具有重要政策启示。

**关键词**：算法治理；平台生态系统；注意力经济；准自然实验；水床效应

## Abstract

While algorithmic recommendation systems optimize user experience, they also trigger concerns about information cocoons and attention monopolization. How to effectively govern algorithms has become a core issue in global digital governance. This study leverages the quasi-natural experiment of China's Qinglang Action algorithm governance policy implemented on November 12, 2024, employing interrupted time series (ITS) and difference-in-differences (DID) methods. Based on high-frequency data of over 253,227 observations from Weibo's trending list (February 2024 - October 2025), we systematically evaluate the restructuring effects of algorithm governance on platform ecosystems. We find: (1) Algorithm governance significantly promotes attention distribution decentralization, with Shannon entropy increasing by 18.7% and HHI decreasing by 11.8%; (2) Risk-based tiered governance leads to content quality differentiation—information gap in social topics decreases by 23.1% while entertainment topics remain stable; (3) Under zero-sum attention constraints, regulation triggers systematic attention reallocation from social to entertainment content, exhibiting a pronounced "waterbed effect"—social topics' share declines by 3.16 percentage points while entertainment rises by 3.84 percentage points; (4) Attention density significantly differentiates across categories, with social topics showing "fewer but refined" and entertainment showing "more and prolonged" patterns. This study proposes theoretical mechanisms of "meta-organization of meta-organizations" and "waterbed effect," extending platform governance theory boundaries and providing new evidence for understanding systemic consequences and unintended effects of algorithm governance, offering important policy implications for improving tiered governance systems.

**Keywords**: Algorithm governance; Platform ecosystems; Attention economy; Quasi-natural experiment; Waterbed effect
